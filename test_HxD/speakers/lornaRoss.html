Lorna Ross

Director of Design, Mayo Clinic Center for Innovation
Bio

Ross has 18 years experience working in design and design research, with the past ten years focused on health and health care. She is a graduate of The Royal College of Art, London, England.

Prior to joining the Center for Innovation at Mayo Clinic as Director of Design, Ross ran the Design for Human Wellbeing Group at the MIT Media Lab Europe. She has led large-scale strategic design projects for the U.S. Department of Defense, Motorola Inc., Interval Research Corporation and Massachusetts General Hospital.

Ross held a faculty position at Rhode Island School of Design in the Industrial Design Department, and has been on the visiting faculty at the National Institute of Design in Ahmedabad, India. She has consulted to the UK Design Council and was a visiting research fellow at University of Reading, United Kingdom, Bionics Group.

At Mayo, Ross and her team of designers are exploring the potential for an embedded design group (located in a live clinical practice) to effect meaningful and sustained transformation within a large, traditional medical institution. They work on behalf of patient and provider communities to effect and evolve the culture of the Clinic but also to determine the most robust model for the integration of design in healthcare.
